Deep learning for MEG classification problems In June 2026 me and my project group for the Master's course Deep Learning applied several complex and state of the art deep learning models on a MEG classification dataset. Without the spatial mapping of the sensors on the participants we were not able to achieve high performance, but we did show the potential of some novel approaches. The article can be downloaded here.

Abstract

The classification of brain states from neuroimaging data is a common problem in neuroscience and clinical research. In this article, we used machine learning to classify different cognitive states from a small set of magnetoencephalography (MEG) recordings. Namely: resting, motor, working memory, and story & maths. We trained 5 models (a baseline, EEGNet, EEGNet-SA, MEG-GPT, and MEG-GCNet), both on a single subject and a cross-subject partition. Multiple models achieved 100% accuracy on the single subject. EEGNet performed best on the cross-subject partition with an accuracy of 64.20%, showing that generalising over multiple subjects remains challenging.
