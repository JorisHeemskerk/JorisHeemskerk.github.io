Environments that sound familiar B-cos of explanations In June 2026 me and my project group for the Master's course Human Centered Machine Learning at Utrecht University wrote a research article examining the applicability of B-cos networks for audio classification. The B-cos transformation is a drop-in replacement for the standard dot-products inside deep learning models. It promises to make models inherently explainable by aligning the weights with the inputs. We transformed environmental audio samples (from the ESC-10 dataset) into log-mel spectrograms and compared a B-cos and normal version of ResNet-18 in terms of predictive performance as well as explainability. The article can be downloaded here.

Abstract

One of the many contributions to the ever-growing body of research surrounding AI explainability is the B-cos transform. This technique aims to align the inputs and weights of neural networks such that inherent explanations can be extracted directly from the model. This article expands upon the research regarding the B-cos transform by assessing to what degree this method carries over to the auditory domain. By comparing this technique to Grad-CAM and LIME using a ResNet-18 model trained on Log-Mel spectrograms, we find that the B-cos transform achieves 90.0% accuracy on the test partition of the ESC-10 environmental sound classification dataset (92.5% on a non B-cos model). When compared to Grad-CAM and LIME, we find B-cos explanations to be quantitatively better (on several metrics) and qualitatively promising. Due to these findings, we deem B-cos explanations a valid approach for spectrogram-based audio classification.
