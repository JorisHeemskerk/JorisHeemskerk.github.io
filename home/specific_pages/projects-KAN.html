Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) In February of 2025 I worked on a short-term research project to look into Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks. These networks are similar to regular Neural Networks, but instead of assigning a set activation function to a layer and learning weights, these networks learn an activation layer per neuron. The ideas is that this increases transparency of the network by showing the way input parameter are related to the predictions. Unfortunately this research project was cut short due to funding restrictions. However, the experiments that were run might still be interesting to share. You can find the most recent version of the article itself here.

WIP abstract

Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) have emerged as a promising alternative to Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) by offering a more interpretable Neural Network structure. Unlike MLPs, KANs replace weight-based connections with learnable activation functions, resulting in a structured decomposition of input features. This paper provides a comparative analysis of KANs and MLPs in terms of accuracy, interpretability, data dimensionality, parameter efficiency, sample efficiency, overfitting, and catastrophic forgetting. We extend previous research by including additional interpretability-focused analyses and adding a new dataset on Sepsis-Associated Delirium (SAD). Our findings indicate that KANs offer competitive accuracy while significantly improving model transparency, making them particularly valuable in applications requiring explainability.
